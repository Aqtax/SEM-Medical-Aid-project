Monthly Medical Tax Credit via Payroll — Employee Guide

Put more cash in your pocket — every month

We will apply your SARS Medical Scheme Fees Tax Credit through payroll each month if you qualify. This reduces your PAYE and increases your net pay — without changing your salary.

Submit your details (Monday.com) Have your membership certificate ready.
Payroll cut-off: 20th of each month. Rollout target: September payroll — please submit by 15 September for inclusion.

Who qualifies?

You pay from your account

You are the principal member on a registered SA medical scheme and you pay the contribution from your own bank account.

You pay for spouse/parent

You pay on behalf of a spouse or parent from your own account, and the principal member will not claim the same credit.

Proof available

You can provide a current membership certificate and proof of payment (bank statement/debit order).

Finance will use the latest SARS medical scheme fees tax credit rates. Rates are reviewed annually.

Current SARS monthly credit rates (from 1 March 2025)

Main member

ZAR 364 per month

First dependant

ZAR 364 per month

Each additional dependant

ZAR 246 per month

Worked example: Family of 4 (you + spouse + 2 children) = 364 + 364 + (2 × 246) = ZAR 1,220 monthly reduction in PAYE; net pay increases by the same amount.

Rates are set by SARS and reviewed annually. Finance updates payroll when SARS publishes changes.

What do I need to upload?

  • Membership certificate listing beneficiaries.
Important for spouse/parent payments: submit the membership certificate now with your request. The confirmation and proof of payment for those contributions must be submitted to SARS when you file your income tax return.

How to apply

  1. Complete the Monday.com form: Submit your details (Monday.com)
  2. Upload the required documents listed above.
  3. Finance reviews within 5–7 business days.
  4. Payroll applies the monthly credit from the first feasible pay run after approval.
Submissions after the 20th will start the following month. For the initial rollout, aim to submit by 15 September to be included in the September payroll.

FAQs

Is this a company contribution?
No — this is a SARS tax credit facilitated via payroll. It reduces PAYE if you qualify.
Will this affect my tax season outcome?
It brings your credit forward monthly. SARS reconciles at assessment; any over/under-claim is handled by SARS.
What if my scheme, beneficiaries, or payer changes?
Notify Finance immediately. Credits will be adjusted from the effective date.
Can both my spouse and I claim the same dependant?
No. Only one taxpayer can claim a dependant in a period. Coordinate to avoid duplicate claims.
